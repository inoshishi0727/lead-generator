# Asterley Bros: Master Drinks Menu Guide

## Purpose
This is the reference document for the AI SDR. When writing outreach emails, the AI pulls seasonal serves, specific suggestions, and distribution info from this guide. It ensures every email includes a credible, timely serve suggestion that shows we understand what bartenders are actually looking for right now.

### Bag in Box (5L BiB)
Available for: SCHOFIELD'S, ESTATE, ASTERLEY ORIGINAL, RED, and ROSÉ.

**Key selling points for bars:**
- Approx **90% reduction** in packaging costs and carbon footprint vs bottles
- **Substantially cheaper per litre** than bottled products
- Helps bars **improve their gross margins** significantly
- Ideal for **high-volume operations** (busy cocktail bars, hotel bars, gastropubs with big Spritz/Negroni/Martini programmes)
- Great for bars focused on **sustainability** (strong story for their customers too)

**Email angle for high-volume venues:** "We also do 5L Bag in Box on our Vermouths and Aperitivo. It's about 90% less packaging waste and substantially cheaper per litre, which really helps with your margins. Worth knowing about if you're going through volume."

**NOTE for AI:** Don't lead with BiB in the first email. Mention it in follow-ups or when a bar shows interest and volume is clearly relevant. The first email should always be about the product and the serve, not the format.

## Product 5: ASTERLEY ORIGINAL British Aperitivo

**ABV:** 12% | **Format:** 500ml bottle + 5L BiB
**Botanicals:** bitter orange, rhubarb, rose, gentian, raspberry, lemon
**Bright, citrusy. Brilliant Campari alternative. Most accessible entry point.**

**AI positioning note — Aperitivo vs Amaro:** ASTERLEY ORIGINAL is an aperitivo (12% ABV) — lighter, brighter, lower ABV. DISPENSE is an amaro (26% ABV) — richer, bolder, higher ABV. It does NOT go into classic Negronis, Boulevardiers, or Manhattans — those need an amaro (DISPENSE) or a sweet vermouth (ESTATE).

## Pre-Batched Negronis

For venues that don't have the time or skill set to make their own Negronis. Ready to pour — just add ice and garnish.

### Classic Negroni
**Format:** 500ml bottle / 5L BiB
Pre-batched using our own vermouth and aperitivo. Consistent quality every time, no measuring, no waste.

### British Rhubarb Negroni
**Format:** 500ml bottle / 5L BiB
A distinctly British twist on the Negroni using rhubarb. Same convenience, a bit of a talking point on the menu.

### Who to pitch pre-batched Negronis to

| Venue Type | Why It Works |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Pubs and gastropubs** | Staff may not be trained bartenders. Pre-batched means consistent quality without the skill set |
| **Hotels (room service / events)** | Speed and consistency matter. No bartender needed at every service point |
| **Event caterers and pop-ups** | No time for cocktail-making in a field or a gallery. Pour and serve |
| **Festival bar operators** | Volume + speed. No measuring, no waste, no training |
| **Restaurant groups** | Centralised procurement, consistent serve across 20+ sites |
| **High-volume bars** | Speed of service during peak hours. Pre-batched supplements the bar team |

**AI positioning note:** Pre-batched Negronis are for venues where convenience, speed, or lack of cocktail expertise is the driver. Don't pitch pre-batched to premium cocktail bars — they'll want to make their own with ESTATE. But for everyone else, this is a genuine time-saver and cost-saver. The BiB format in particular is brilliant for volume.

**Email angle:** "We also do pre-batched Negronis — a Classic and a British Rhubarb — in 500ml bottles or 5L Bag in Box. If your team doesn't always have time to batch their own, these are ready to pour over ice. Consistent every time and great margins on the BiB."

## Multi-Product Serves (Cross-Selling Opportunities)

These serves use two or more Asterley Bros products. Great for increasing the order size.

| Serve | Products Used | Season | Notes |
|-------|-------------|--------|-------|
| **The Asterley Bros Negroni** | ESTATE + DISPENSE + Gin | Evergreen | The flagship cross-sell: our vermouth + our amaro |
| **Boulevardier** | ESTATE + DISPENSE + Bourbon | Autumn / Winter | Same two-product story, richer base spirit |
| **Americano** | ESTATE + DISPENSE + Soda | January / Low ABV | Light, low ABV, two products |
| **Hanky Panky** | BRITANNICA + ESTATE + Gin | Evergreen | Three Asterley Bros products in one glass |
| **Pink Negroni** | ASTERLEY ORIGINAL + ESTATE + Gin | Spring / Summer | Lighter twist using the aperitivo instead of amaro |
| **Paper Plane** | DISPENSE + ASTERLEY ORIGINAL + Bourbon + Lemon | Autumn / Winter | Modern classic, two products (amaro + aperitivo) |

**Important distinction for AI:** DISPENSE (amaro, 26% ABV) goes into Negronis, Boulevardiers, and Americanos. ASTERLEY ORIGINAL (aperitivo, 12% ABV) goes into Spritzes, Pink Negronis, and lighter twists. They are not interchangeable — an aperitivo is lighter and lower ABV, an amaro is richer and bolder. Using the wrong one changes the cocktail fundamentally.

**Email angle for multi-product serves:** "The great thing is that a few of our bottles work together. A Negroni with ESTATE and DISPENSE, or a Hanky Panky with BRITANNICA and ESTATE. Two or three of our products in one glass and your customers get a proper story with it."

## Vocabulary Guide (for AI)

**DO say:** mainstream styles, classic styles, classic Vermouth options
**DON'T say:** continental styles, continental anything

**DO say:** use it in their House Negroni
**DON'T say:** house Negroni spirit

**DO say:** Pinot Noir base
**DON'T say:** Pinot Noir grape base

**DO say:** delicious, banging, brilliant, gorgeous, amazing
**DON'T say:** measured, refined, curated, artisanal (too corporate)
