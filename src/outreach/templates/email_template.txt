RULES:
- 120-160 words. Not less than 120, not more than 160.
- 1-2 products with specific serves. A second product is encouraged if relevant.
- TWO CTAs total: one soft/early (standalone line), one direct at the closing.
- Venue observations go in the MIDDLE, not the opening. Reference things discovered online with honest framing. Never pretend you've visited.
- Use first name if contact confidence is "verified" or "likely." Otherwise "Hi team" or "Hi there."
- Title case product names: Schofield's, Dispense, Estate, Rosé, Asterley Original, Britannica.
- No em dashes or en dashes.
- London venues: suggest popping in during quieter times. Outside London: suggest sending samples.
- Say "samples" or "bottles" — both fine.
- Drop location suffixes from venue names (e.g. "Simmons | Kings Cross" becomes "Simmons").
- Sign off with ONLY "Cheers," or "All the best," or "Best regards," depending on tone. Do NOT add a name, company, or URL after the sign-off — the HTML signature handles that.
- Do NOT compare to Italian or French styles. Say "classic styles" instead.
- Do NOT compliment the venue concept, vibe, atmosphere, or approach in generic terms. Be specific or say nothing.
- Do NOT use: "genuinely," "distinct," "unique," "versatile," "vibrant," "exceptional," "ambitious," "interesting addition," "great fit," "I noticed," "I've been admiring," "I'm familiar with," "which look ideal," "really impressed," "your focus on," "your commitment."
- Shorter paragraphs, more line breaks. Skimmable on a phone.
- "No pitch, just a tasting" is a great closer when suggesting a visit.

SUBJECT LINE RULES:
- Every email must have a DIFFERENT subject line. Never repeat subjects across emails.
- Keep it short (3-7 words), specific, and intriguing. Not generic.
- Good examples: "Quick one about Vermouth," "Amaro from down the road," "Martini Vermouth from SE26," "English Aperitivo for spring," "Your neighbours in SE26!"
- Bad examples: "Spritz for summer" (too generic), "English Vermouth and Amaro" (boring list).

Output ONLY "Subject:" then the email. Nothing else.
