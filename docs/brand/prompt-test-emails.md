# AI SDR Prompt Test: 10 Sample Outreach Emails (V5)
## Asterley Bros Stockist Acquisition

**Date:** 8 March 2026
**Status:** Current — this is the definitive sample email reference.
**Supersedes:** V4 (voice rules still correct, but V4 used older email structure)

### What changed from V4

1. **Opening line:** Now leads with who we are + why we're writing. Not venue flattery.
2. **Early CTA:** Every email has a soft CTA in the first 2-3 lines ("Can we send samples?" / "I'd love you to try..."). The early CTA is its own standalone line, separated by blank lines above and below.
3. **Venue observations:** Moved to the middle of the email, woven in naturally. Not front-loaded.
4. **White space:** Shorter paragraphs, more line breaks. Skimmable on a phone. The early CTA and the sign-off block each get a blank line above them.
5. **Two CTAs:** Soft/early + direct/closing. If someone only reads the first two lines and the last line, they still know what we want.
6. **Sign-off:** Just "Rob" (not Rob Sherwood).
7. **Location:** SE26 (not "SE London").
8. **Visit timing:** Always suggest quieter times. Never weekends. Rarely past 6pm. Bars are too busy to talk then. Weekday afternoons, early evenings before the rush, or "when you've got a quieter moment."

### Rules applied (unchanged from V4)

**Voice:** Bartender-to-bartender. Punchy, warm, enthusiastic. Not corporate. Genuine hyperbole welcome. No em dashes. Ever. Product names capitalised. Vermouth and Amaro capitalised.

**Personalisation:** Never pretend you've visited. Reference things discovered online with honest framing. Email is a precursor to visiting in person.

**Contact names:** Only use first names when confirmed (owner with daily presence, or very recent press/IG). Default to "team" or "Hi there."

**Seasonal awareness:** It's March. Spring/Summer menus being planned or launching. Lead with spritzes, highballs, White Negronis, light serves. Evergreen classics as secondary.

**Vocabulary:** "Classic styles" not "continental." "Pinot Noir base" not "Pinot Noir grape base." "Use it in their House Negroni" not "house Negroni spirit."

### Rob's example email (the benchmark)

> Hi Tom,
>
> Saw the TimeOut piece recently and it looked great! Congrats!
>
> I was hoping to drop in and say hello next week if you were around?
>
> Quick intro: I'm Rob from Asterley Bros, makers of English Vermouth and Amaro based in SE London. We make a delicious English Dry Vermouth called SCHOFIELD'S (created with bartenders Joe and Daniel Schofield) that is designed to make the ultimate Martini (and a banging White Negroni too!). Crisp and herbaceous with a distinctly British character. Quite different from other classic Vermouth styles, and I thought it might work well in any upcoming Spring menus you were working on.
>
> I'd love to swing by, try one of your stirred-down classics, and leave you with some samples to play with. No pitch, just a tasting.
>
> When's a good time to catch you?
>
> Cheers,
>
> Rob
> Asterley Bros
> asterleybros.com

**Note:** This benchmark predates the structure refinement. In V5, the opening would lead with identity + hook rather than the venue observation. But the voice, energy, and warmth are the target for every email.

### Universal email structure (V5)

```
Subject: [Short, specific, intriguing]

[Greeting],

[1-2 sentences: Who we are + why we're writing. Warm, direct, genuine.
Contains a soft CTA or core proposition.]

                            ← line break

[1 sentence: Early CTA. "Can we send samples?" / "I'd love you to try..."
This is its own standalone line.]

                            ← line break

[2-3 sentences: Product detail. Specific products, serves, reasons
they're relevant. Scannable.]

[1-2 sentences: Venue observation. Genuine, specific, natural.
NOT front-loaded.]

[Optional: 1 sentence on format/supply if relevant]

[1 sentence: Closing CTA. "When's good?" / "What's the best way
to set that up?" Always suggest quieter times: weekday afternoons,
before the rush. Never weekends, rarely past 6pm.]

                            ← line break

[Sign-off]

Rob
Asterley Bros
asterleybros.com
```

**Visit timing rule:** When suggesting dropping in, always frame it for a quieter time. Never weekends. Rarely past 6pm. Bars are too busy to talk then. "Pop in one afternoon," "swing by before the rush," "catch you on a quiet weekday" all work.
