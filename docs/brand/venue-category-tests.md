# SDR Venue Category Test: Taxonomy, Targets, and Sample Emails

## Purpose
This document tests the AI SDR's ability to categorise different venue types and generate tailored first-contact emails for each. It builds on the V4 email rules and the Master Drinks Guide to demonstrate that the system can adapt its tone, product focus, and CTA across all venue categories.

**Date:** 8 March 2026
**Season:** Spring (March). Menus changing. Spritzes, highballs, White Negronis, light serves.
**Rules:** All V4 rules apply. See SDR_Prompt_Test_10_Sample_Emails_V4.md.

---

### Key Observations

1. **ASTERLEY ORIGINAL is the gateway product** across almost every category. It's accessible, makes a simple serve (Spritz), and has the broadest appeal. This should be the lead product for new category launches.

2. **DISPENSE is the prestige product** that works in serious cocktail contexts. It carries the most interesting story (24 botanicals, Pinot Noir base, London meets Sicily) and commands respect in professional bar settings.

3. **SCHOFIELD'S opens doors in premium settings** where Martini programmes matter: hotel bars, airline lounges, membership clubs. The Schofield brothers collaboration adds credibility.

4. **BRITANNICA and ROSÉ are follow-up products**, not first-email leads. They work best once a relationship is established or in very specific contexts (specialist retail, summer campaigns).

5. **BiB (Bag in Box) becomes a selling point** in categories 8-11 (events, groups, festivals) where volume, margin, and simplicity matter. It should be mentioned in first emails for these categories, unlike for single venues.

6. **Tone scales with formality of the buyer**. A bartender in Bethnal Green gets "Cheers, Rob." A spirits buyer at British Airways gets "Best regards, Rob." The AI SDR needs to detect this from the category and adjust automatically.

7. **The "family Amaro recipe / Sicilian wife" story** is a powerful differentiator for Italian-leaning venues and any context where the personal story matters (membership clubs, luxury retail, experiences).
